  • Wheel Alignment

    Suspension alignment is essential for keeping your car safe, predictable and comfortable. During an alignment we adjust the angles of the wheels to match factory settings so the vehicle tracks straight and every tyre shares the workload. The main angles we set are camber, caster and toe.

    Camber is the tilt of the wheel when viewed from the front. Too much negative camber wears the inner edge of a tyre; too much positive camber scrubs the outer edge. Setting camber correctly helps the car grip in corners while keeping tyre wear even.

    Caster describes the forward or rearward tilt of the steering axis. More positive caster builds straight-line stability and self-centering, while too little can make the steering nervous. We set caster so the car feels planted without becoming heavy or wandering on the motorway.

    Toe looks at whether the wheels point toward or away from each other. Excessive toe-in or toe-out drags the tyres and wastes fuel. Proper toe keeps the car steady and minimises scrub on the road.

    Checking alignment regularly protects tyre life, fuel economy and ride quality. Impacts such as potholes, kerb strikes or suspension work can knock the geometry out, so it pays to have it measured periodically instead of waiting for a vibration or uneven tread.

    We can also create custom alignment settings for track days, comfortable daily driving or show stance so the handling matches your exact needs.

  • Pre-Purchase Inspections

    A pre-purchase inspection is a crucial step in the process of buying a used car. This comprehensive assessment, conducted by one of our experienced technicians, aims to evaluate the overall condition of the vehicle before finalising the purchase. During the inspection, various aspects of the car are examined, including the engine, transmission, brakes, suspension, tires, and electrical systems. Additionally, the technician will check for any signs of previous accidents, rust, or hidden damage that may not be immediately apparent. The goal of this inspection is to provide you with a clear understanding of the vehicle's current state, uncover any potential issues that could lead to costly repairs in the future, and ultimately ensure that you are making a well-informed decision. Investing in a pre-purchase inspection can save you from unexpected headaches and financial burdens, making it a wise move for any prospective owner.

    After the PPI is carried out you will be sent a full report consisting of a digital vehicle health check consisting of photos and a walk around video of the vehicle. Also you will receive a full diagnostic report.
